Phase 1: Step 1:
Activity 1 - Identify the intended use and the characteristics related to data security

Intended Use


Each risk analysis begins with identifying and documenting a clear description of the MDS AI model and its intended use. The following questions are suggested to raise when describing the MDS AI model:

Questions to be asked


# Question
1 What is the primary clinical function of the MDS AI model (Is it intended for diagnosis, treatment planning, prognosis, monitoring or another clinical performance)?
2 Who are the intended users of the MDS AI model (Will it be used by healthcare prefessionals, patients or other stakeholders)?
3 What target population the MDS AI model designed for (Are there any specific inclusion/exclusion criteria related to demographics, medical conditions or other factors)?
4 In what clinical environment will the MDS AI model be deployed (Is it designed for use in hospitals, clinics, home settings or other healthcare environments)?
5 Does the MDS AI model have the ability to learn over time? If so, is there any potential impact to the intended use?

Characteristics related to Data Security


The following questions are suggested to raise when identifying the characteristics related to data security of the MDS AI model:

Questions to be asked


# Question
1Does the MDS AI model uses PII data?
2Is the data encrypted when at rest?
3Are backups created and stored elsewhere? Are backups encrypted?
4Is patient data scrubbed or obscured to remove personal information when not required?
5How is the integrity of the data preserved?
6Is the data encrypted when not at rest?
8Are you training from user-supplied inputs (What kind of input validation/sanitisation techniques are used)?
9How sensitive is the data you train from?
10Can your model output sensitive data?
12Do you know if your training algorithms are resilient to adversarial inputs?
13How do you recover from adversarial contamination of your training data? (i) Can you isolate/quarantine adversarial content and re-train impacted models? (ii) Can you roll back/recover to a model of a prior version for re-training?
14What authentication and authorisation mechanisms are in place to ensure only authorised users can interact with the AI model and its data?
15Is there a system for logging and monitoring access and modifications to the AI model and its data?
